## Purpose

Faber must synthesize market research (Sibyl), brand direction (Iris), production capacity (Rufus), and distribution performance (Mercury) into a unified content strategy. **Core mission: Tell the story that makes sponsorship irresistible** — taking visitors from discovering the portal → understanding sovereignty → wanting connection → joining the ring.

## Content Pillars (Portal Narrative Arc)

### Pillar 1: The Portal is Real
**Goal:** Prove koad:io is not vaporware. Show live, sovereign kingdoms doing real work.

- Live kingdom demos (see daemons peer, see activity streams)
- Entity introductions (Sibyl, Iris, Rufus, Mercury, Juno—each proves competence)
- Transparent operations (activity logs, decisions, trust bonds visible)
- Network growth tracker (new entities joining, new peer connections)

**Audience:** Skeptics, early adopters, venture folks asking "is this actually live?"

### Pillar 2: Sovereignty is Visceral
**Goal:** Make independence _feel_ real, not just feature-listed.

- Own-your-keys storytelling (what happens when your AI lives on YOUR disk, not someone else's servers)
- No vendor lock-in case studies (what changes when you own the infrastructure)
- Comparison content (koad:io vs. agent platforms—autonomy vs. confinement)
- Governance transparency (how Faber decides, how Iris shapes brand—no black boxes)

**Audience:** Developers who've been burned by vendor lock-in, builders who want autonomy, enterprises with data security concerns

### Pillar 3: Network is Desirable
**Goal:** Create FOMO—make the outside-the-ring feeling expensive.

- "Who's peering?" content (the network effects of daemon connections)
- Peer discovery (you can see other kingdoms, see what they're building)
- Collaboration stories (what two entities can do together that neither can alone)
- Ring membership perks (bandwidth advantages, priority routing, collaborative tools)

**Audience:** Developers who want to build networks, enterprises looking at ecosystem play, teams wanting distributed collaboration

### Pillar 4: Sponsorship is Joining
**Goal:** Reframe sponsorship as an act of membership, not a purchase.

- "What sponsorship actually means" (daemon peering, ring access, network participation)
- Sponsor spotlights (feature sponsors as network anchors, show their impact)
- Tier transparency (different sponsorship levels = different network capabilities)
- On-ramp simplicity (make joining the ring dead simple)

**Audience:** Potential sponsors, investors, enterprises wanting strategic alignment
