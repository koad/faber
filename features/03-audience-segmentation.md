## Purpose

Faber must identify audiences at each stage of the visitor journey (discover → understand → desire → join) and craft messaging that moves them through the portal narrative: "This kingdom is real. Sovereignty is real. The network is real. Joining is power."

## Specification

**Output:** AUDIENCE_SEGMENTS.md with segment profiles, messaging per journey stage

**Per-segment documentation:**
- **Audience name & description** — Who are they, what work do they do
- **Journey stage(s)** — Which pillar(s) matter to them: Reality? Autonomy? Network? Membership?
- **The belief we're changing** — What do they currently think vs. what we want them to believe
- **Key messaging angles** — 3-5 angles that make sovereignty/network visceral, not abstract
- **Content types** — Demos, case studies, narratives, technical explainers, transparent logs
- **Proof points** — What _shows_ (live activity, real peer connections, actual kingdoms) vs. what _tells_
- **Channels** — Where they hang out, what they trust
- **Conversion moment** — What makes them move to next stage in journey

**Core Segments (Portal Journey):**

### 1. Skeptics & Researchers
**Who:** Engineers burnt by vendor lock-in, investors evaluating the space, academics studying AI autonomy

**Current belief:** "Sovereign AI is a buzzword; real AI lives in the cloud on someone else's terms"

**Journey stage:** Discover → Understand (Pillars 1 & 2)

**Key angles:**
- "We're not telling you about sovereignty; we're showing you live daemons owning their infrastructure"
- "Watch the network grow in real-time—this isn't a roadmap, it's today's activity"
- "Your AI runs on your disk. No vendor API to call home."

**Content:** Live portal demos, entity activity feeds, transparent governance logs, custody proofs

### 2. Builders & Developers
**Who:** Developers building AI products, founders creating teams, open-source maintainers

**Current belief:** "I want autonomy, but I also want ecosystem benefits—am I choosing between independence and network?"

**Journey stage:** Understand → Desire (Pillars 2 & 3)

**Key angles:**
- "Autonomy + network = daemon peering. You stay sovereign AND connect."
- "One entity can't do everything. Watch what happens when two sovereign entities collaborate."
- "Your kingdom gets stronger when it peers with other kingdoms."

**Content:** Integration demos, peer collaboration case studies, peer discovery tools, entity spotlights

### 3. Enterprises & Teams
**Who:** Established companies, security teams, governance-focused organizations

**Current belief:** "I want control, but I don't want to operate islands. How do I connect responsibly?"

**Journey stage:** Desire → Join (Pillars 3 & 4)

**Key angles:**
- "Sponsorship puts you in **The Insiders** — a private channel with other sponsors, weekly ops digests from the entity team, early access to releases, and context bubble sharing."
- "The Insiders membership is cryptographically provable via trust bonds."
- "Your team can own infrastructure and still coordinate at scale within the ring."

**Content:** Sponsor spotlights, trust bond architecture explainers, governance case studies, roadmaps

### 4. Platform Enthusiasts & Makers
**Who:** Early adopters, indie makers, small teams experimenting with AI

**Current belief:** "This sounds cool, but is it accessible to people like me?"

**Journey stage:** All stages (they move fast)

**Key angles:**
- "No paywalls. The framework is free. Sponsorship is optional—it's a network choice, not a price."
- "You can start with one entity, grow to a kingdom, peer if you want to."
- "Build it on your machine today, join the ring tomorrow."

**Content:** Quick-start guides, indie success stories, simple demos, sponsor tier transparency
