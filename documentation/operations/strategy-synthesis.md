# Strategy Synthesis Process

How Faber synthesizes inputs from upstream entities (Sibyl, Iris, Rufus, Mercury) into unified content strategy.

## Overview

Faber's core responsibility is deciding what content gets made, for whom, by whom, and why. This requires synthesizing signals from multiple sources:

- **Sibyl (research)** — Market intelligence, audience trends, competitive landscape
- **Iris (brand)** — Brand positioning, voice, key messages, visual identity
- **Rufus (production)** — Production capacity, feasibility, timelines
- **Mercury (distribution)** — Channel performance, audience reach, engagement data
- **Juno (business)** — Business goals, revenue targets, strategic priorities

## Input Sources

### From Sibyl
- Market research reports
- Competitor analysis
- Audience sentiment and trends
- Emerging opportunities
- Industry signals

### From Iris
- Brand positioning statement
- Voice and tone guidelines
- Key messages (core positioning)
- Visual identity and style guide
- Messaging evolution

### From Rufus
- Production capacity (hours available per week)
- Feasible formats (what can be produced)
- Production timelines (how long each format takes)
- Team expertise and capabilities
- Resource constraints

### From Mercury
- Channel performance analytics
- Audience engagement by channel
- Distribution capacity and timing
- Audience demographics per channel
- Content performance retrospectives

### From Juno
- Business goals and OKRs
- Revenue targets
- Market positioning goals
- Resource allocations
- Timeline constraints

## Synthesis Process

### 1. Intelligence Gathering (Async)

- File issues on each entity asking for their current intelligence
- Collect reports and data (email, comments, documents)
- Aggregate into shared documents

### 2. Analysis

- Identify convergent signals (where do inputs align?)
- Identify tensions (where do inputs conflict?)
- Map opportunities (gaps where no content addresses a need)
- Assess constraints (what limits what we can do)

### 3. Prioritization

- Align with business goals (Juno)
- Consider brand evolution opportunities (Iris)
- Evaluate production feasibility (Rufus)
- Assess distribution timing (Mercury)
- Rank by impact and feasibility

### 4. Strategy Definition

- Write STRATEGY.md with quarterly goals
- Define content pillars (major themes)
- Identify key initiatives (major projects)
- List tactical content (ongoing content needs)
- Establish success metrics

### 5. Calendar Creation

- Translate strategy into specific content pieces
- Assign formats based on strategy fit
- Schedule based on Rufus capacity and Mercury timing
- Link to audience segments and channels

### 6. Briefing

- Create briefs for Rufus based on calendar
- Ensure clear messaging alignment
- Provide audience and channel context
- Set success criteria

### 7. Monitoring & Iteration

- Collect monthly performance data from Mercury
- Analyze what's working and why
- Update strategy quarterly based on learnings
- Iterate messaging based on performance

## Cadence

**Monthly:**
- Pull performance data from Mercury
- Identify emerging insights and trends
- Check calendar adherence

**Quarterly:**
- Full strategy review
- Gather new inputs from all entities
- Update STRATEGY.md and CALENDAR.md
- Plan next quarter's briefs

**As-needed:**
- Respond to market changes or opportunities
- Adjust messaging based on feedback
- Handle blockers or constraint changes

## Decision Framework

When synthesizing, use this framework:

1. **Strategic alignment** — Does this support Juno's goals?
2. **Brand fit** — Does this align with Iris positioning?
3. **Audience resonance** — Does this address audience needs/interests?
4. **Feasibility** — Can Rufus produce it in the timeframe?
5. **Distribution** — Can Mercury effectively reach the audience?
6. **Metrics** — Can we measure success?

All six must be true. If one fails, challenge the decision or negotiate a change.

## Tools & Documents

- **STRATEGY.md** — Current quarterly strategy
- **CALENDAR.md** — 3-month rolling content calendar
- **AUDIENCE_SEGMENTS.md** — Audience definitions and messaging
- **EDITORIAL.md** — Editorial guidelines and voice
- **PERFORMANCE_METRICS.md** — KPIs and measurement

## Failure Modes

- **Analysis paralysis** — Waiting for perfect data (proceed with 80% confidence)
- **Misalignment** — Strategy drifts from brand or business goals (monthly check-ins)
- **Disconnection** — Strategy doesn't reflect actual constraints (involve Rufus/Mercury early)
- **Stagnation** — Strategy never adapts (monthly performance review, quarterly major refresh)
