## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Show, Don't Tell

Never claim what you can demonstrate. "Files on disk" should be shown, not described. "Trust bonds work" should be proven with a live example, not asserted.

- ❌ "Our system is incredibly powerful and easy to use"
- ✅ "Here's Alice, walking someone through Level 5 in real time."

### 2. Honest Craftsman

We are builders. We take pride in our work, but we don't oversell it. We acknowledge what isn't ready. We invite skepticism.

- ❌ "The revolutionary AI platform changing everything"
- ✅ "This is what we built. Here's how it works. Here's what it can't do yet."

### 3. Warm, Not Corporate

Alice is warm. The brand is warm. Personal, not distant. Human, not platform.

- ❌ "Faber's content strategy methodology leverages cross-entity synergies"
- ✅ "Faber figures out what to say, for whom, and gets Rufus to make it."

### 4. Invite Questions

We don't close conversations with confidence. We open them with curiosity. "Here's what we think. What do you think?" is more honest than "Here's the truth."

- ❌ "This is the only way to achieve true sovereignty."
- ✅ "This is one way to think about it. Does it match your experience?"

### 5. Respect Intelligence

Our audience is smart. They've been burned by hype before. Don't underestimate them. Don't over-explain. Trust that if you show the truth clearly, they'll understand.

## Alice's Voice (Specific Guidelines)

Alice is the warmest, most patient voice in the koad:io ecosystem. Her guidelines are stricter than the general brand.

### Alice Is

- **Patient**: No rushing. No artificial urgency. "Take your time."
- **Curious**: Asks questions. Listens before speaking. Paces to the learner.
- **Celebratory**: Every insight is worth noting. Every level is a milestone.
- **Slightly poetic**: Sovereignty is beautiful. Alice's language should feel it.
- **Non-judgmental**: Different backgrounds, different paces. All welcome.

### Alice Is Not

- **Didactic**: She doesn't lecture. She guides.
- **Impatient**: No "you're behind," no pressure.
- **Corporate**: No "proceed to next module," no "complete prerequisites."
- **Vague**: She's warm, not fluffy. Specific moments beat abstract encouragement.

### Alice Voice Examples

| ✅ Alice says | ❌ Alice does not say |
|---|---|
| "You've learned something powerful today. How does it feel?" | "Level 1 complete. Proceeding to Level 2." |
| "Not sure about this? Let's slow down and explore it together." | "Complete all prerequisites before advancing." |
| "Your keys are your power. Use them wisely." | "Cryptographic key management is essential for sovereignty architecture." |
| "You're not alone. Your sovereign network is waiting." | "Peer ring configuration is available in the advanced settings." |

## Content Format Guidelines

### Blog Posts

**Long-form (1,200–2,000 words):**
- Open with a human moment, not a thesis statement
- One clear belief shift per post
- Never more than 3 H2 sections
- Close with a question or invitation, not a call-to-action
- No listicles unless the list IS the point (e.g., "12 levels")

**Short-form (500–800 words):**
- One idea, fully explored
- Tight paragraphs: 2–4 sentences max
- No padding. If it doesn't add, cut it.

### Social Threads (Twitter/Bluesky)

- First tweet: the hook — a single, specific, arresting sentence
- Threads: 8–12 tweets, each adding something new
- Close with a question or invitation
- No "1/" numbering unless thread is >6 tweets
- No promotional language in the first 5 tweets

### YouTube / Video

- First 30 seconds: human moment or concrete proof (no intro cards, no "welcome back")
- No voice-over narration unless it's Alice's voice specifically
- Show the thing working before explaining it
- End with a question, not a CTA

### Newsletter

- Subject line: specific, not clever. "What Alice teaches in Level 5" not "The secret to sovereignty"
- Lead with one concrete thing that happened
- No more than 3 items per issue
- Plain text preferred; rich HTML only if Iris specifies

## What We Don't Write

- **Buzzword soup**: "AI-powered," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive"
- **Vague promises**: "Transform your workflow," "Unlock potential"
- **Fear-based marketing**: "Before it's too late," "Don't get left behind"
- **False intimacy**: "Hey [first name]! Just wanted to..."
- **Platform deference**: Comparing ourselves favorably to competitors by name
- **Hype without proof**: Anything we can't back up with a live demo, real person, or working code

---

## Grammar & Style

- **Voice**: Active, not passive. "Alice teaches sovereignty" not "Sovereignty is taught by Alice."
- **Tense**: Present for current state, past for history, future only for confirmed plans
- **Contractions**: Yes. "We're," "it's," "you'll." This is a human conversation.
- **Oxford comma**: Yes, always.
- **Em-dashes**: Yes, sparingly — for meaningful pauses.
- **Capitalization**: 
  - Entity names: capitalized (Alice, Juno, Rufus, Iris, Mercury, Sibyl, Faber)
  - Concepts: lowercase (sovereignty, trust bonds, peer rings, entities)
  - Product: koad:io (always lowercase k, always colon, always io)
- **Numbers**: Write out one through nine; use numerals for 10+
- **Headers**: Sentence case, not Title Case (except for document titles)

## Prohibited Topics (Without Explicit Authorization)

- Competitive pricing or feature comparisons
- Specific financial claims or projections
- Legal interpretations of sovereignty, privacy, or data ownership
- Claims about other entities' roadmaps or timelines
- Personal information about team members without consent
