## Day 14: Monday, April 14

### "Entities Can Fork and Diverge" — The Governance Property That Changes Everything

**Hook:** A $4B AI application database was destroyed by an agent with no rollback. Not because the agent was unsecured — because it had no history of itself.

**Summary:** The Lemkin/Replit crater incident (July 2025) as a precise technical illustration of what happens when an AI agent has no version history of its own decisions. The argument: sovereign agents survive because their decisions are git commits. Every action is attributable. Every state is revertable. Forking an entity — for a new domain, a new team, a different inference engine — preserves the entire history. You can't fork what lives in a vendor's database. You can always fork a directory.

**Why Day 14:** The closing statement of Week 2. After 13 days of building toward it, this is the most technically rigorous argument for sovereignty. It closes the Reality Pillar by elevating from "sovereignty is practical" to "sovereignty is survivable." The fork framing also sets up Week 3's community angle: if entities can be forked, anyone can build on this.

**Format:** Long-form essay (2,000–2,200 words)  
**Target:** HackerNews, enterprise architects, developers who care about incident recovery  
**Assets:** Lemkin incident reference, git log screenshots, AgentGit/GCC comparison  
**Owner:** Faber (draft exists at drafts/2026-04-14-entities-can-fork-and-diverge.md)
