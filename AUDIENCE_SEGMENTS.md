## Segment: Alice's First Students (Primary Funnel)

**Who**: Curious humans skeptical of tech solutions, seekers of autonomy and freedom

**What they want**:
- To understand sovereignty without jargon
- A human guide, not a platform
- Personal attention and pacing they control
- Proof that this is real and trustworthy

**Messaging**:
- "Come meet Alice. She'll teach you what sovereignty actually means."
- "12 levels, at your pace, no pressure."
- "A guide who listens. Not a platform that listens."

**Content formats**:
- Narrative: Who is Alice?
- Video: Alice's warmth and philosophy
- Testimonial: People going through levels
- Walkthroughs: Level-by-level progression

**Success metric**: "I want to meet Alice" comments and PWA signups

## Segment: Independent Journalists (NEW — Truth Pillar)

**Who**: Investigative reporters, newsletter writers, Substack creators. People who already feel the brokenness of platform journalism.

**What they want**:
- Stories that cannot be edited by platforms
- Proof of what they report (verifiable reasoning, not just conclusions)
- Distribution they own
- A way to share *how they think*, not just what they concluded

**Pain points**:
- Platform journalism flattens nuance → headlines get taken out of context
- No way to show readers "here's the step-by-step thinking that led to this story"
- Substack is owned distribution but still lacks provenance
- "Fact-checking" is binary (true/false) when journalism is actually "here's how I came to understand this"

**What koad:io offers**:
- **Context bubbles**: Render your investigation as a playlist. Show how you thought through the story.
- **Verifiable narratives**: Readers can replay your reasoning. This story cannot be misquoted.
- **Peer rings**: Connect with other journalists who value verifiable truth.
- **Cryptographic proof**: Alice signs your work. This happened. Here's the proof.

**Messaging**:
- "Your investigation deserves to be verifiable."
- "What if your readers could see how you came to this conclusion?"
- "Journalism that can't be edited. Stories that can't be misquoted."
- "Context bubbles: The infrastructure for truth in reporting."

**Content formats**:
- Essay: "What context bubbles mean for reporting"
- Case study: The $200 laptop story as a context bubble (koad's 7-day genesis, verifiable)
- Tutorial: How to render your investigation as a playlist
- Interview: Journalists who tried context bubbles
- Messaging framework: "Trust without authorities"

**Where they hang out**:
- Twitter/Bluesky (journalism/misinformation threads)
- Substack (their own newsletters)
- Mastodons & independent platforms
- Press freedom organizations
- Journalism schools

**Success metric**: Independent journalists publishing context bubble investigations, "This is how I understand journalism now" testimonials

## Segment: Builders & Infrastructure Folks (Secondary)

**Who**: Indie developers, DevOps engineers, infrastructure tinkerers. People who want to own their stack.

**What they want**:
- Sovereignty, not lock-in
- Transparent governance (code on disk, not vendor platform)
- Tools to build peer systems
- Technical depth and documentation

**Messaging**:
- "Your infrastructure deserves to be yours."
- "Entities, trust bonds, sovereign operations."

**Content formats**:
- Technical deep-dives
- Architecture documentation
- How-to guides (trust bonds, peer rings)
- Code examples

**Success metric**: Builders integrating koad:io, entity networks expanding

## Segment: Enterprise & Governance (Secondary)

**Who**: IT directors, compliance officers, governance advocates. Organizations concerned with transparency, vendor lock-in.

**What they want**:
- Decentralized governance that's still auditable
- Transparency and control
- Scalability without vendors
- Regulatory compliance

**Messaging**:
- "Governance that scales with trust, not authority."
- "Files on disk. Decisions in git. Sovereignty by design."

**Success metric**: Enterprise pilots, case studies on governance outcomes
